Tab 1 — Overview
Core identification, contact information, and billing preferences
Customer Type
Required
Type
Radio (Business / Individual)
Default
Business
Used in
Invoices, Reports, GST
Classifies the customer as a Business (company, firm, LLP) or an Individual (person). This drives GST treatment defaults, display name logic, and reporting.
💡
Use Case: Set to Business for B2B invoicing (GSTIN required). Set to Individual for B2C (no GSTIN). Affects GSTR-1 reporting — B2B invoices above ₹2.5 lakh are reported separately.
GST Treatment
Required
Type
Pill selector (5 options)
Default
Registered Business
Used in
GST, Tax computation, GSTR
The most important tax classification field. Controls which tax rules apply to this customer, which fields are shown/required, and how GST is computed on their invoices. Options: Registered Business · Unregistered Business · Overseas · SEZ · Consumer.
💡
Use Case: Choosing Registered Business makes GSTIN mandatory and enables CGST/SGST or IGST split. Overseas applies zero-rated IGST. SEZ applies 0% GST with mandatory GSTIN. Consumer means B2C — only GST total is needed. See the GST Treatment Reference section for full rules.
⚠️ Changing GST Treatment after invoices have been created does not retroactively update existing invoices. Always set this before raising the first invoice.

Emails, Letters, PDF
The honorific prefix for the primary contact. Appears in email communications, formal letters, and printed invoices when addressed to a person.
💡
Use Case: Used when sending personalised payment reminders or invoice emails — e.g. "Dear Dr. Sharma". Most relevant when Customer Type is Individual.

The registered legal company or organisation name. This appears on invoice header and address blocks. Separate from the Display Name which is used for searching.
💡

Display Name
Required
Type
Text input
Saved as
customer_name
Used in
Everywhere (primary identifier)
The name shown on all invoices, sales orders, quotes, reports, and dropdowns throughout the application. This is the primary identifier for the customer.
💡
Use Case: This appears in the "Customer" column on every Sales Invoice, Sales Order, Quote, Payment, and Report. Keep it short and recognisable — e.g. "TCS" rather than the full legal name.
⚠️ This is the only mandatory field in the customer form. The record cannot be saved without it. It is also used as the document name (primary key).
GSTIN / Tax ID
Conditional
Format
15 chars (e.g. 27AAPFU0939F1ZV)
Visible for
Registered, SEZ
Used in
GSTR-1, B2B invoices
The 15-character GST Identification Number issued to registered businesses. Auto-converts to uppercase. Format: SS-PPPPPNNNNPC-Z-C where SS=state code, P=PAN, N=entity number, C=checksum.
💡
Use Case: Printed on B2B invoices as legally required. Used in GSTR-1 B2B section. Required for ITC claim by the customer. The first 2 digits (state code) also auto-derive Place of Supply for intra-state tax split.
⚠️ Validated against the official GSTIN pattern. Will show "Invalid GSTIN format" error if not matching. Required when GST Treatment = Registered Business or SEZ. Hidden for Overseas, Unregistered, Consumer.
PAN Number
Conditional
Format
10 chars (e.g. ABCDE1234F)
Visible for
Registered, Unregistered, SEZ
Used in
TDS, Compliance, 26AS
Permanent Account Number — the 10-character alphanumeric identifier issued by the Income Tax department. Auto-converts to uppercase. Embedded within GSTIN (chars 3–12).
💡
Use Case: Required for TDS deduction at source. Used in annual TDS returns (Form 26Q). Also used for 26AS reconciliation and high-value transaction reporting above ₹50 lakh. Hidden for Overseas and Consumer customers.

The primary email address for the customer. Used as the default recipient when emailing invoices, payment receipts, account statements, and payment reminders.

Currency
RequiredAuto-filled
Default
INR
Options
INR, USD, EUR, GBP, AED…
Used in
All invoices for this customer
The default billing currency for this customer. All invoices, sales orders, and quotes created for this customer will default to this currency. Amounts are stored in the base company currency (INR) with exchange rate applied.
💡
Use Case: For a US client, set currency to USD. Invoices will be raised in USD, and the exchange rate at time of invoice is used to record the INR equivalent in the ledger. Critical for multi-currency reporting and forex gain/loss calculation.
⚠️ Once an invoice is created, changing the customer currency does not affect existing invoices. Always set the correct currency before creating the first invoice.
Payment Terms
Optional
Type
Dropdown
Options
Net 7/15/30/45/60, Due on Receipt, EOM
Used in
Invoice due dates, Aging
The default payment terms for this customer. Automatically pre-fills the due date on new invoices based on the invoice date. Drives the AR Aging report buckets.
💡
Use Case: A customer with "Net 30" terms — an invoice raised on 1st April will auto-set due date to 1st May. Overdue invoices appear in the "30–60 days" bucket in the Aging report. Can be overridden per invoice.
Credit Limit
Optional
Type
Number (₹)
Default
0 = unlimited
Used in
Risk controls, Alerts
The maximum outstanding receivable amount allowed for this customer. When outstanding balance exceeds this limit, a warning is shown when creating new invoices. Set to 0 for no limit.
💡
Use Case: For a customer with a history of late payments, set a credit limit of ₹5,00,000. If their outstanding invoices exceed this, the sales team is warned before raising new invoices — reducing credit risk.

Tab 2 — Address
Billing address (printed on invoices) and shipping address (for delivery challans)
Address Line 1 & 2
Optional
Scope
Billing address
Used in
Invoice PDF, E-way Bill
GST relevance
State used for tax split
The street address of the customer's billing location. Line 1 is typically street/building; Line 2 is area/landmark. Printed verbatim on the "Bill To" block of PDF invoices.
💡
Use Case: Required for a legally valid GST invoice. The customer's billing address (city, state) determines whether tax is CGST+SGST (intra-state) or IGST (inter-state). Also used for E-Way Bill generation above ₹50,000.
City, State, Pincode
Optional
State
Dropdown (all Indian states)
Pincode
6-digit postal code
Used in
GST tax split, Delivery
City, State, and Pincode of the billing address. The State field is critical for GST compliance — it determines CGST+SGST vs IGST split when compared with the company's state.
💡
Use Case: If your company is in Maharashtra (State 27) and the customer's state is also Maharashtra → CGST 9% + SGST 9%. If the customer is in Karnataka → IGST 18%. The Pincode is used for courier and delivery challan generation.
Country
Optional
Default
India
Used in
Export invoices, Currency
GST relevance
Non-India = zero-rated IGST
The country of the billing address. For Overseas customers, must be set to the correct non-India country. Drives export invoice classification and forex reporting.
💡
Use Case: For a US client, set Country to "United States" and GST Treatment to "Overseas". This marks the invoice as an Export Invoice, applies zero-rated IGST, and the invoice is reported in GSTR-1 under "Export" section.
Shipping Address (Lines 1–2, City, State, Pincode, Country)
Optional
Default
Same as billing if blank
Used in
Delivery Challan, E-way Bill
GST relevance
Ship-to state for tax
The physical delivery address for goods. If left blank, the billing address is used. When filled, this address appears on the Delivery Challan and E-Way Bill as the consignee address.
💡
Use Case: A Mumbai head office (billing) with a Pune warehouse (shipping). The shipping state determines the correct GSTIN state code on the E-Way Bill. Also used if the "Ship-to" state differs from "Bill-to" for branch supply scenarios.

Tab 3 — Other Details
Tax compliance, customer source, and opening balance
Place of Supply
Conditional
Visible for
Registered, Unregistered, SEZ
Options
All 38 GST state codes
Used in
GSTR-1, Tax computation
The state where the supply is deemed to have taken place under GST law. Determines CGST+SGST (intra-state) vs IGST (inter-state) on invoices. Pre-filled from GSTIN state code where possible. Uses official GST state codes (e.g. 27-Maharashtra).
💡
Use Case: A registered customer in Delhi (07) buying services from your Mumbai company — Place of Supply = 07-Delhi → IGST applies. If both are in Maharashtra → CGST+SGST. This is a legal requirement on every B2B GST invoice. Hidden for Overseas and Consumer customers.
⚠️ Required when GST Treatment is Registered Business, Unregistered Business, or SEZ. Invoices without correct Place of Supply may be rejected during GSTR-1 filing.
Customer Source
Optional
Type
Dropdown
Options
Cold Call, Referral, Website…
Used in
Sales reports, CRM
Records how this customer was acquired — Cold Calling, Email, Existing Customer, Partner, Campaign, Website, Referral, Word of Mouth, or Other. Used for marketing effectiveness analysis.
💡
Use Case: Track which marketing channels generate the most revenue. Filter the customer list by Source in reports to see revenue by acquisition channel — e.g. "Referral customers account for 40% of ARR".
Opening Balance
Optional
Type
Number (₹)
Default
0
Used in
Trial Balance, Ledger
The outstanding receivable balance owed by this customer on the Books opening date (migration date). Creates a journal entry in the Accounts Receivable ledger to carry forward existing debt.
💡
Use Case: When migrating from another accounting system, existing customers may already owe money. Enter ₹2,50,000 as Opening Balance for a customer with a pre-existing debt. This ensures the Trial Balance is correct from Day 1 in Books.
⚠️ Only set this during initial data migration. Setting this after transactions have begun can cause imbalance in the ledger.

Bank Name
Optional
Type

Payment reference
The name of the bank where the customer holds their account. Used as reference when receiving payments or when issuing refunds to the customer.
💡
Use Case: When receiving NEFT/RTGS payments from this customer, the bank name helps verify the source. Also used when issuing credit note refunds — the finance team knows which bank to transfer to.
Account Number
Optional
Type
Numeric (monospace)
Stored
Encrypted / masked
Used in
Refunds, Reconciliation
The customer's bank account number. Displayed in monospace font for readability. Used as reference for fund transfers and reconciliation of incoming payments.
💡
Use Case: When a customer requests a refund against a credit note, the finance team uses this account number to process the NEFT transfer without needing to ask the customer again.
IFSC Code
Optional
Format
11 chars (e.g. HDFC0000001)
Auto
Converts to uppercase
Used in
NEFT/RTGS transfers
Indian Financial System Code — uniquely identifies a bank branch in India's electronic funds transfer system. Required for NEFT and RTGS transactions. Auto-converted to uppercase on input.
💡
Use Case: Required when processing NEFT/RTGS refunds to the customer's bank. The IFSC + Account Number together uniquely identify the destination account for any electronic fund transfer.

Tab 5 — Remarks
Internal notes visible only to your team
Internal Notes
Optional
Type
Multi-line textarea
Visible to
Internal team only
Used in
Customer notes, History
Free-form internal notes about this customer. Never shown to the customer on invoices or statements. Used by sales, accounts, and support teams to store context about the relationship.
💡
Use Case: "Customer prefers invoices via email by 1st of month. Disputes all invoices with rounding differences. Key contact: Priya +91 98xxx. Payment usually on 45th day despite Net 30 terms. Escalation contact: CFO Ravi Sharma."
ℹ️
Privacy note: These notes are internal only. They do not appear on any customer-facing documents, emails, or portal views. Safe to record payment behaviour, communication history, and sensitive business notes.

GST Treatment Reference
How field visibility and requirements change based on selected GST Treatment
GST Treatment GSTIN PAN Place of Supply Tax Applied GSTR-1 Section
Registered Business Required ✓ Optional Required ✓ CGST+SGST or IGST B2B Invoices
Unregistered Business Hidden Optional Required ✓ CGST+SGST or IGST B2CL / B2CS
Overseas Hidden Hidden Not applicable IGST 0% (zero-rated) Exports (with/without payment)
SEZ Required ✓ Optional Required ✓ GST 0% (zero-rated) SEZ Supplies
Consumer (B2C) Hidden Hidden Not applicable CGST+SGST or IGST B2CS (Small) / B2CL (Large >2.5L)
⚠️
GSTR-1 Impact: B2B invoices (Registered Business) are reported with invoice-level detail including GSTIN. B2CS invoices (Consumer, Unregistered) are consolidated by state. Export invoices (Overseas) are reported in a separate section. Always select the correct treatment to ensure accurate GST returns.
